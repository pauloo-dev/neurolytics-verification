Beyond the Blueprint: Teachers' Adaptive Agency in Inclusive Education under Kenya's CBE
Reflexive Thematic Analysis · Braun & Clarke (2006) · Semi-structured interviews · Nakuru County
7 Themes 9 Participants
Analysis Overview
Qualitative interview study exploring how primary teachers in Nakuru County exercise adaptive agency to include neurodivergent learners within Kenya's Competency-Based Education framework.
Research Question 1How do teachers perceive and enact inclusive practices to maximise inclusive utility (presence, participation, progression) for neurodivergent learners in CBC classrooms?
Research Question 2What adaptive strategies and institutional conditions enable or constrain teachers' efforts to maximise inclusive utility under CBC in Kenyan public primary schools?

T1
Adaptation Ensembles: Assembling the Feasible Frontier
UDL · Social Constructivism · Inclusive Utility
Teachers assemble context-specific bundles of low-cost adjustments — adaptation ensembles — calibrated to what is feasible within constraints. Key components include heterogeneous grouping, translanguaging, visual mediation, task chunking, peer scaffolding, and flexible pacing. These emerge from teachers' situated pedagogical judgement, not curriculum mandate.

T2
Inclusive Utility in Practice: Reading the 3Ps
Inclusive Utility (3P) · IPAA (Florian, 2014)
Teachers demonstrate a working awareness of Presence, Participation, and Progression even without the terminology. Presence is near-universal in urban schools but fragile in rural contexts. Progression is most often read through small wins and observable behavioural change — a tentative alignment with CBE's competency logic over examination metrics.

T3
Structural-Ecological Tensions: The Borrowed Architecture Problem
Ecological Systems Theory · Policy Borrowing (Phillips & Ochs, 2003)
Kenya's CBE was structured after Nordic models assuming a tripartite support system, small classes (18–21), and universal assistive technology. None are present here. Class sizes reach 90, assistive tech is almost entirely absent, and teachers use personal smartphones and data bundles. The clustering system continues to reproduce merit-based stratification while CBE publicly disavows it.

T4
Lateral Collaboration as Lifeline: Cooperation Without Infrastructure
Ecological Systems (mesosystem) · Mulholland & O'Connor (2016)
In the absence of vertical Nordic-style support, teachers default to lateral peer-to-peer collaboration. WhatsApp groups serve as de facto professional infrastructure. Subject panels, co-teaching (found in two schools), and cross-school networks supplement this. However, what is described is largely cooperation rather than true collaboration — it lacks joint problem-solving, role clarity, and reciprocal accountability.

T5
Teacher Adaptive Agency as Moral Vocation
VBE (Ngwacho, 2024) · DisCrit · Teacher Agency (Cong-Lem, 2021)
Across all nine participants, adaptation for inclusion is framed not as professional obligation but as moral calling — "ni moyo" (it is the heart). Teachers invest personal time, finances, data bundles, and emotional labour beyond any institutional expectation. This is simultaneously a strength and a systemic risk: it masks structural deficits and is not scalable.

T6
Neurodivergence: Between Recognition, Resourcelessness, and the Strength-Turn
Neurodiversity paradigm (Armstrong, 2012) · Social constructivism · DisCrit
All participants describe learners with features of autism, ADHD, dyslexia, dysgraphia, or sensorimotor difficulties — yet almost none have formal diagnostic support. Teachers identify, assess, and respond without specialist help. A key emergent finding is a spontaneous strength-based turn: teachers consistently leverage learner strengths (art, sports, oral fluency) as adaptive strategy, prefiguring the neurodiversity paradigm's core claim.
Identification without diagnosisDeficit vs strength framingParent denial/resistanceMedical model referralStrength-based observation

T7
The CBE Paradox: Aspirational Design, Enacted Contradiction
Policy Borrowing · Ecological Systems (macro) · VBE
Teachers endorse CBE's inclusive aspirations broadly while documenting a persistent implementation gap. The curriculum remains entangled with 8-4-4 logic — examination pressure, merit-based stratification, and content overload persist. The pathway system is good policy encountered as non-functioning in practice, particularly in rural Cluster 4 contexts.

1 — Adaptation Ensembles
13 codes
ADAPT :: heterogeneous_grouping
Deliberate mixing of ability levels within groups, rejecting streaming in favour of peer-assisted collaboration.
9/9
ADAPT :: translanguaging
Use of Kiswahili, Kikuyu, or other languages to scaffold concepts taught/assessed in English. Individual initiative, not policy; sometimes hidden from administration.
5/9
ADAPT :: visual_multimodal
Projectors, pictorials, wall charts, drawings, videos (often downloaded at own expense) to supplement verbal instruction.
7/9
ADAPT :: task_chunking
Breaking tasks into smaller sequential units; simplified instructions; step-by-step delivery; reduced question complexity.
8/9
ADAPT :: peer_pairing
Deliberate pairing of a struggling learner with an understanding peer; social scaffolding without extraction from class.
7/9
ADAPT :: seating_proximity
Positioning of neurodivergent/struggling learners close to the teacher's desk, away from windows/doors. Primary low-cost spatial accommodation.
6/9
ADAPT :: scaffolding_ido_wedo_youdo
Gradual release of responsibility: teacher models → co-constructs → releases. Named explicitly by T03; referenced implicitly by others.
6/9
ADAPT :: flexible_extra_time
Completing tasks after lesson; oral alternatives to timed writing; doubling time for reading assessments.
7/9
ADAPT :: oral_alternatives
Allowing verbal response, dictation, or drawing as accommodation for learners with writing/motor difficulties.
4/9
ADAPT :: practical_handson
Real-world activities (chicken coops, cooking, field trips, everyday objects) to make abstract concepts concrete.
8/9
ADAPT :: music_songs
Songs, rhymes, musical patterns as mnemonic and engagement tools. Notably used in T02's poetry lesson; highly effective for reluctant learners.
4/9
ADAPT :: modelling_clay_objects
Physical manipulation of objects (clay, number shapes, letter forms, containers) to develop fine motor skills and make abstract symbols tangible.
3/9
ADAPT :: ICT_personal_phone
Teacher's personal smartphone used to search, download, or display digital content. Represents a hidden personal cost borne by teachers.

2 — Inclusive Utility (3P)
6 codes
3P :: presence
Physical attendance and affective sense of belonging. Urban: near-100%. Rural: seasonal variability driven by farm labour and poverty.
9/9
3P :: participation
Active engagement with content, materials, or peers. Presence ≠ participation — several teachers note learners physically present but mentally disengaged.
9/9
3P :: progression
Forward movement, however small. Measured through small observable wins (new word read, extra sentence written, skill mastered) more than test scores.
9/9
3P :: celebrating_small_wins
Explicit affirmation of micro-milestones as motivational and relational tools. Strongest in T04 (SNE) and T09 (Grade 1).
6/9
3P :: belonging
Emotional safety and social acceptance as preconditions for participation. Teachers describe creating environments where learners "feel part and parcel."
7/9
3P :: dropout_disengagement
Withdrawal from schooling. Rural: higher rates. T05: ~2% dropout; T08: seasonal attendance; T05: silent Grade 9 withdrawal.

3 — Structural-Ecological Conditions
9 codes
STRUCT :: class_size
Range: 31 (SNE) to 90 (T01, lower primary). All mainstream participants report overcrowding as a primary constraint. Nordic baseline of 18–21 never approached.
9/9
STRUCT :: resource_scarcity
Absence of basic materials (lab equipment, assistive devices, printing, supplementary learning materials). Teachers routinely improvise or self-fund.
9/9
STRUCT :: technology_gap
Absence of functioning digital infrastructure. Government tablets non-functional in most schools. Wi-Fi absent in rural contexts. Personal phones fill the gap at personal cost.
8/9
STRUCT :: retooling_inadequacy
Insufficient depth, coverage, and continuity of CBE teacher PD. T05 estimates ≈20% effective retooling after nearly 10 years.
6/9
STRUCT :: assessment_pressure
High-stakes KJSEA logic persists alongside CBE's formative rhetoric, pushing teachers back toward examination drilling.
8/9
STRUCT :: clustering_contradiction
Cluster school system (C1–C4) reproduces merit-based stratification under new labels, contradicting CBE's anti-ranking stance.
5/9
STRUCT :: social_worker_absence
No professional social work function in Kenyan public schools. Administration absorbs this role informally, creating gaps in school-family-community coordination.
2/9
STRUCT :: teacher_wellbeing
Mental health impacts of workload, paperwork, and emotional labour. T01 describes dedicated TPD session on stress management.
3/9
STRUCT :: leadership_variability
Head teacher stance as primary enabler or barrier. T04 contrasts obstructive vs supportive heads; T08 identifies leadership renewal as the single most needed change.
6/9
4 — Collaboration
6 codes
COLLAB :: whatsapp_lateral
Informal teacher-initiated WhatsApp groups for resource sharing and problem-solving. Characterised by unstructured content and limited feedback mechanisms.
7/9
COLLAB :: subject_panels
Formal departmental meetings (typically once per term). Attendance and engagement variable.
7/9

Two teachers simultaneously in classroom. Found in two schools (T07, T09). Enables differentiated support for learners with additional needs.

School-family interaction for learner support. Highly variable: supportive in urban schools, resistant or absent in rural/low-income contexts.

4/9

5 — Teacher Agency & Moral Vocation
5 codes
AGENCY :: child_first_philosophy
Learner-centred motivation overriding institutional constraints, exhaustion, and lack of resources. Core moral driver of adaptive behaviour.
9/9
AGENCY :: personal_financial_investment
Teachers spending personal funds on classroom materials, printing, data bundles, tools. SNE allowance (Ksh 10,000) often reinvested in learners.
8/9
AGENCY :: vocation_heart
"Ni moyo" — teaching as moral/spiritual calling. Strongest in SNE context (T04) but present across all participants.
7/9

Arriving early, staying late, weekend work, lunch-time tutorials — all without compensation. Coexists with legally discouraged extra tuition.
8/9

Active improvisation and workaround generation (field trips to workshops, coop construction, downloading videos from cyber cafés) in the face of structural absence.

6 — Neurodivergence Recognition
5 codes
NEURO :: identification_no_diagnosis
Teachers recognise and name neurodivergent profiles in the absence of formal diagnostic support. Identification through sustained observation, not clinical assessment.
7/9
NEURO :: strength_based_framing
Spontaneous teacher attention to what a learner does well. Art, music, sports, oral fluency, memory emerge as competency domains where textual learners struggle.
6/9
NEURO :: parent_denial
Parental refusal to accept SEN identification, attributed to stigma, misinformation, and preference for mainstream placement.
5/9
NEURO :: medical_model_referral
Referral pathway: teacher → head → parent → hospital → CSO → placement. Slow, bureaucratic, rarely completed in practice.
3/9
NEURO :: labelling_effects
Awareness of how labels follow learners between classes and schools, shaping teacher expectations and peer relations. T01 articulates this explicitly re: ADHD.

7 — CBE Paradox
5 codes
CBE :: aspirational_vs_enacted
Gap between CBE's published inclusive aspirations and classroom-level reality. Present in all nine interviews.
9/9
CBE :: hybrid_system
CBE contains substantial 8-4-4 logic — assessment pressure, content overload, merit-based placement. T01's term "hybrid" captures this precisely.
7/9
CBE :: pathway_promise_vs_reality
Talent pathway system endorsed in principle but non-functional in rural contexts where designated schools lack facilities, teachers, and enrolment.
4/9
CBE :: parental_8-4-4_mindset
Parents' residual examination-oriented expectations conflict with CBE's project-based demands. Creates friction around materials, grading, and school choice.
6/9
CBE :: goodwill_despite_constraints
All teachers express genuine belief in CBE's potential despite enumerated failures — a double-consciousness of systemic critique and personal commitment.

Analytic Memos

T1 (Adaptation Ensembles) — Universal across all 9. Rural teachers rely more on practical/hands-on and translanguaging; urban teachers have broader ICT access.
T3 (Structural Tensions) — Strongest in rural and SNE contexts (T04, T05, T08). Urban teachers experience it differently: more infrastructure but still face assessment pressure.
T4 (Collaboration) — Co-teaching model (T07, T09) substantially elevates depth. Most others operate in informal lateral-only modes.
T5 (Teacher Agency) — Universal motivational substrate. Notably highest in longest-serving teachers (T01, T07) and SNE specialist (T04).

Theoretical Integration Note

Inclusive Utility (3P) is the central analytical construct, receiving inputs from all seven themes. Themes 1 & 5 are the primary positive drivers at the classroom level (microsystem). Theme 3 constrains the feasible frontier from exo- and macrosystem levels, while Theme 4 functions as a partial compensatory mechanism — shifting the efficient frontier within constraints. Themes 6 & 7 are cross-cutting lenses that reveal the normative and political dimensions of what might otherwise appear as purely pedagogical decisions.
