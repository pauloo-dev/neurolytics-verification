Analysis Dashboard
Reflexive thematic analysis of nine semi-structured interviews with primary teachers in Nakuru County, Kenya, exploring adaptive agency for neurodivergent learners under CBE.
Research Question 1How do mainstream primary teachers in Nakuru County assemble and deploy low-cost adaptation ensembles to support neurodivergent learners under CBE, and what pedagogical reasoning shapes those in-the-moment choices?
Research Question 2How do teachers evaluate the inclusive utility (presence, participation, and progression) of their adaptations, and what trade-offs influence these judgements?
Research Question 3To what extent do within-school collaboration practices and wider ecological conditions enable or constrain teachers' adaptive agency under CBE?

Inclusive Utility (3P) — Central Construct
Derived from the expected utility framework in financial economics: inclusive utility U = f(Presence, Participation, Progression), where each P is a measurable dimension of educational access. Teachers act as adaptive agents optimising utility subject to structural constraints — the "feasible frontier" is the set of achievable 3P combinations given class size, resources, and policy conditions.
Presence
Physical attendance + affective belonging. Precondition for all else. Near-universal in urban; fragile in rural due to farm labour and poverty.
High across sample (9/9 address)
Participation
Active engagement with content, materials, peers. Presence does not guarantee participation. Belonging is the mediating variable.
Strong across sample (9/9 address)
Progression
Observable forward movement, however small. De-linked from exam scores. Micro-milestones reframe what counts as educational achievement.
Variable; context-dependent (9/9)

Analytic Notes on Matrix Patterns
T1 (Adaptation Ensembles) is universal. Rural teachers rely more heavily on translanguaging and hands-on; urban teachers have broader ICT access.
T3 (Structural Tensions) is most elaborated in rural and SNE contexts. Urban teachers experience it differently: infrastructure exists but assessment pressure remains high.
T4 (Collaboration) is dramatically elevated where co-teaching exists (T07, T09). All others operate in informal lateral-only modes.
T6 (Neurodivergence) is weakest in T01 and T05, both senior school teachers where learner profiles may be less visible or attributed to motivation rather than cognition.

Theoretical Integration Note
Inclusive Utility (3P) is the central analytical construct receiving inputs from all seven themes. Themes 1 and 5 are the primary positive drivers at the classroom level (microsystem). Theme 3 constrains the feasibility frontier from exo- and macrosystem levels. Theme 4 is a partial compensatory mechanism that shifts the frontier within constraints. Themes 6 and 7 are cross-cutting lenses revealing the normative and political dimensions of what might otherwise appear as purely pedagogical decisions. The IU construct draws on expected utility theory from financial economics: teachers act as agents maximising educational utility U = f(P1,P2,P3) subject to a feasibility frontier defined by structural conditions.
